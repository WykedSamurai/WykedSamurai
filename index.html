Why Wyked Samurai Exists

Wyked Samurai has been part of the story since 2018.

What began as a creative identity grew into a larger question: why can't tabletop RPGs be used in corporate settings?

HR is looking for communication, leadership, trust, perspective-taking, adaptability, and problem-solving. Tabletop roleplaying builds those skills constantly.

Wyked Samurai began with the belief that games like Dungeons & Dragons, Pathfinder, and other tabletop roleplaying systems are more than entertainment. They are structured environments where people practice judgment, collaboration, communication, conflict navigation, and empathy.

As Penny studied Human Resources, gamification, people analytics, and workplace behavior, the idea grew. A consulting company using TTRPGs for workplace development was only the beginning. The deeper problem was that hiring and professional development still rely on outdated systems. We think we can understand a person from a résumé. We cannot.

People now learn from countless sources, build skills in unconventional ways, and pursue careers through paths that do not always fit traditional formats. At the same time, workplaces are becoming more disconnected and hostile because people are losing the ability to understand one another.

Wyked Samurai exists because people need better ways to learn through experience, reflect on that growth, prove what they can do, and build workplaces rooted in understanding.

Our Philosophy

Information is not understanding.

Information can tell someone that a stove is hot. Experience is what teaches the lesson. That same principle applies to leadership, teamwork, conflict, judgment, and professional growth.

Experience

People learn through meaningful action, safe failure, feedback, and reflection.

Evidence

Capability should be visible through demonstrated behavior, not only claimed on paper.

Empathy

Perspective-taking is not a soft extra. It is a core workplace skill.

Evolution

Technology should strengthen human cognitive processes, not replace human development.
