Evidence Behind the Work

Academic research supporting experiential learning and human-centered systems.

Wyked Samurai is shaped by academic work in gamification, globalization, human behavior, and workplace culture. The research behind this work examines gamification not as a gimmick, but as a system that can influence motivation, learning, identity, power, equity, and workplace behavior.

Why this matters

The ideas behind Wyked Samurai are not only creative. They are grounded in research, coursework, professional reflection, and workplace experience.

Penny Carter's academic work explored how game-based systems affect motivation, workplace culture, institutional power, employee identity, ethics, and HR practice.

Professional framing

Wyked Samurai uses the language of experiential learning, structured scenarios, evidence-based development, workplace behavior, systems thinking, and human-centered design.

The tabletop influence is part of the methodology, but the professional focus is people, systems, skill development, and demonstrated capability.

Research Themes

What the coursework supports

Gamification as Structure

Gamification is more than points, levels, badges, or rewards. It can shape how people engage, learn, participate, and respond to systems.

Ethical Design

Game-based systems can motivate people, but they also raise questions about fairness, manipulation, surveillance, privacy, and cultural fit.

Workplace Behavior

Gamified workplace tools can influence motivation, identity, productivity, collaboration, competition, and the way employees experience authority.

Global Awareness

Different cultures may respond differently to competition, recognition, ranking, collaboration, and reward systems.

HR Relevance

The research connects directly to human resources, training, employee engagement, inclusive policy design, and ethical workplace systems.

Evidence-Based Growth

Wyked Samurai's strongest positioning is structured experience, demonstrated skill, ethical engagement, and people-centered systems.

How We Apply It

From research to practical design

The concepts under Wyked Samurai are informed by research into gamification, experiential learning, and workplace systems. Rather than treating game mechanics as surface-level engagement tools, Wyked Samurai approaches them as structured experiences that can shape behavior, build skills, reveal decision-making patterns, and support more human-centered professional development.

This approach supports projects such as Wyked Samurai Consulting and Fabled Compass, where the goal is to help people demonstrate capability through evidence, realistic scenarios, reflection, and growth over time.

Evidence Points

For companies, owners, and recruiters

Academic Foundation
